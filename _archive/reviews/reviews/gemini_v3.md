### 🛠️ Areas for Refinement Prior to Submission

While the core of the paper is outstanding, there are a few structural and contextual adjustments you should make to satisfy TRSE reviewers:

**1. The Missing "Figure 1"**

- **The Issue:** Your manuscript directly references Figure 2, Figure 3, Figure 4, and Figure 5 in the text and the Appendix. However, there is no mention of a Figure 1 anywhere in the document.
- **The Fix:** You either need to renumber your figures so they start at 1, or insert the missing Figure 1 if it was accidentally omitted from this draft.

**2. Contextualizing for an International Audience**

- **The Issue:** TRSE has a broad, international (though heavily US-based) readership. While you mention that the Weimar Republic scenario resonates with Czech historical experiences of democratic fragility, a non-European reviewer might need slightly more handholding.
- **The Fix:** Add two or three sentences in the "The Czech Educational Context" section explicitly detailing _why_ the Weimar Republic is a standard curricular unit in Czech 9th-grade classrooms. How does it bridge to their understanding of subsequent Czech history (e.g., the Munich Agreement)?

**3. Deepening the Pedagogical Implications**

- **The Issue:** TRSE reviewers typically look for strong bridges between research and classroom practice.
- **The Fix:** In the "Implications for History Education" section, consider expanding on the practical reality of the knowledge–HPT nexus. If HPT is deeply knowledge-dependent (as your findings confirm), what is your advice for a high school teacher grading an HPT assessment? Should they assess perspective-taking and historical knowledge together with a single rubric, or tease them apart?

**4. Navigating the "KSA-3" Exploratory Finding**

- **The Issue:** The finding that authoritarianism negatively correlates with presentism is fascinating (the _rigidity_ pathway). However, because it was not preregistered and barely skirts the edge of significance (_p_ = .016) before Bonferroni correction, reviewers might see it as over-interpreted.
- **The Fix:** You have already correctly flagged it as an exploratory finding that requires cautious interpretation. To be entirely safe, you might want to explicitly state in the discussion that while this suggests an avenue for future research regarding cognitive rigidity, it does not alter the primary confirmatory conclusions of the study.
