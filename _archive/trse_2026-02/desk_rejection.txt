Dear Dr. Juda Kaleta:

Thank you for your submission to Theory & Research in Social Education. I have had the opportunity to read your manuscript, and while the topic of historical perspective taking is interesting, I am afraid that it would not be reviewed favorably by external reviewers. Therefore, I have decided not to send it out for review for the following reasons:

--The rationale for the study is not clear, in terms of teaching and learning. It is an expectation that submissions to TRSE need to not only report trends in the field, but also expand and create knowledge, adding to our field’s understanding of social studies teaching and learning.
--More background on the use and application of the Hartmann and Hasselhorn standardized HPT assessment, both in the Czech Republic, and around the world would be helpful. Moreover, what is the rationale for exploring ideology's association with performance on the assessment?
--The manuscript should draw significant conclusions for how social studies education researchers should apply the knowledge you developed.
--A distinct literature review would help the reader understand your study in the context of relevant studies on HPT.
--TRSE readership expects scholarship published in the journal to be grounded in theory. This would require a separate Theoretical Framework section positioned before the Method section. The manuscript would also need to conduct data analysis with that theoretical lens in a manner that is made explicit in the Discussion section.
--The manuscript, particularly the findings, is highly technical, leaving the reader wondering what the nature of the findings are. In your Discussion, you summarize the findings, but the reader will want to grasp what the results reveal about your analysis of the assessment.

I understand that the decision not to send your manuscript out for review is disappointing, but hopefully this will allow you to find a more suitable outlet for your work more quickly. You are of course now free to submit the paper elsewhere should you choose to do so. Your manuscript may be better suited to one of Taylor & Francis’ other journals, and the Taylor & Francis editorial team might be in touch with some specific suggestions.

I hope this decision will not deter you from submitting manuscripts to Theory & Research in Social Education in the future.

Sincerely,

Anne-Lise Halvorsen
Editor, Theory & Research in Social Education
annelise@msu.edu
