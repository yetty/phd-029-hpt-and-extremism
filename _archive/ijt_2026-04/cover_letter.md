Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript titled **"Cross-Cultural
Validation and Ideological Fairness of a Historical Perspective
Taking Instrument: Evidence from Czech Secondary Students"** for
consideration in the *International Journal of Testing*. As
suggested by the editorial office of *Applied Measurement in
Education*, we believe this manuscript aligns closely with IJT's
core interest in cross-cultural test adaptation and measurement
fairness.

**Summary.** This study reports the first Czech adaptation of the
Hartmann and Hasselhorn Historical Perspective Taking (HPT)
instrument and the first investigation of whether politically
charged assessment content introduces construct-irrelevant variance
related to students' ideological attitudes. Using data from 293
secondary students across 10 schools, we integrate CFA, IRT-based
differential item functioning analysis, and multi-group CFA for
measurement invariance testing. The results demonstrate full scalar
invariance across ideology groups, no differential item
functioning, and a convergent/discriminant validity pattern
consistent with the intended score interpretation.

**Fit with IJT.** The manuscript addresses topics central to IJT's
scope: cross-cultural instrument adaptation following ITC
guidelines, DIF methodology applied to a novel grouping
variable (political ideology), and measurement invariance testing
with ordinal data. The primary contribution is a transferable
protocol for evaluating whether attitudinally charged assessment
content introduces construct-irrelevant variance---a question that
extends beyond history education to any domain where sensitive
content raises construct contamination concerns.

**Novelty.** To our knowledge, no prior study has examined whether
ideological attitudes introduce measurement bias in any historical
thinking instrument. The finding that a scenario involving political
extremism functions with full measurement equivalence across
ideology groups has practical implications for assessment design in
politically sensitive domains.

**Preregistration.** The study design, hypotheses, and analysis plan
were preregistered on the Open Science Framework prior to data
collection (https://osf.io/yng37/). De-identified data and analysis
scripts are publicly available.

**Open Access.** Charles University holds a CzechELib Read & Publish
agreement with Taylor & Francis, which covers the article processing
charge for open access publication.

**Confirmations.** The manuscript is not under consideration
elsewhere. The author has no competing interests to declare. Ethical
standards for non-interventional educational research were followed;
participation was voluntary and all data were de-identified.
