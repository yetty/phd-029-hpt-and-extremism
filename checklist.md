# Academic Manuscript Quality Audit Checklist

**Version:** Comprehensive (General → Domain-Specific → Journal-Calibrated)
**Use case:** Empirical research in education / social sciences (adaptable to HPT + ideological contamination study)

---

# I. Foundational Coherence & Conceptual Integrity

## 1. Problem Formulation

- [ ] Is the central research problem stated in one precise, falsifiable sentence?
- [ ] Does the introduction distinguish clearly between:
  - phenomenon
  - construct
  - instrument
  - theoretical threat?

- [ ] Are key constructs explicitly defined (not assumed)?
- [ ] Is there conceptual separation between:
  - theoretical concern
  - empirical test
  - normative implications?

- [ ] Is the contribution identifiable within the first 2–3 pages?

**Red flag:** The paper oscillates between theory-building and instrument validation without clarity.

---

## 2. Theoretical Coherence

- [ ] Are constructs situated within a recognized theoretical tradition?
- [ ] Are competing conceptualizations acknowledged?
- [ ] Is conceptual slippage avoided (e.g., authoritarianism ≠ extremism ≠ motivated cognition)?
- [ ] Are normative claims clearly distinguished from descriptive claims?
- [ ] Are epistemic assumptions explicitly stated?

**Red flag:** Literature is cited, but not synthesized into a structured argument.

---

## 3. Literature Positioning

- [ ] Are foundational sources cited (not only recent derivatives)?
- [ ] Is the review analytical rather than enumerative?
- [ ] Are disagreements in the field acknowledged?
- [ ] Are citations proportionate to claim strength?
- [ ] Is overreliance on one theoretical camp avoided?

**Red flag:** Claims supported only by secondary citations.

---

# II. Research Design Integrity

## 4. Hypotheses & Inference Logic

- [ ] Are hypotheses derived deductively from theory?
- [ ] Is the direction of expected effects justified?
- [ ] Are boundary conditions specified?
- [ ] Are null results theoretically meaningful?
- [ ] Is confirmatory vs exploratory analysis clearly separated?

---

## 5. Sampling & External Validity

- [ ] Is sampling strategy transparent?
- [ ] Are inclusion/exclusion criteria reported?
- [ ] Is sample representativeness addressed explicitly?
- [ ] Are clustering structures acknowledged?
- [ ] Are demographic distributions reported adequately?

**Red flag:** Sample described as “diverse” without quantification.

---

## 6. Statistical Power & Design Adequacy

- [ ] Is sample size justified (a priori or sensitivity analysis)?
- [ ] Is clustering appropriately modeled?
- [ ] Are design limitations openly discussed?
- [ ] Is model complexity proportionate to N?

---

# III. Measurement & Construct Validity

## 7. Instrument Transparency

- [ ] Are all instruments described in sufficient detail?
- [ ] Are original sources cited?
- [ ] Is adaptation process documented (translation, item selection)?
- [ ] Are scoring procedures transparent?
- [ ] Are reverse-coded items clearly reported?

---

## 8. Reliability Evidence

- [ ] Internal consistency (α and/or ω) reported?
- [ ] Subscale reliabilities reported?
- [ ] Reliability interpreted cautiously?
- [ ] Are floor/ceiling effects examined?

---

## 9. Factor Structure & Dimensionality

- [ ] Is dimensionality tested appropriately?
- [ ] EFA/CFA justified?
- [ ] Model fit indices reported?
- [ ] Factor loadings interpretable?
- [ ] Cross-loadings discussed?

---

## 10. Measurement Invariance / Bias

- [ ] Is measurement invariance tested across relevant groups?
- [ ] Is DIF examined when group comparisons are central?
- [ ] Are partial invariance decisions justified?
- [ ] Are conclusions limited to invariant parameters?

**Critical for HPT contamination design.**

---

# IV. Statistical Analysis Quality

## 11. Model Specification

- [ ] Is model choice theoretically justified?
- [ ] Are fixed and random effects justified?
- [ ] Are assumptions tested?
- [ ] Are effect sizes reported?
- [ ] Are confidence intervals included?

---

## 12. Transparency & Reproducibility

- [ ] Are analytic decisions documented?
- [ ] Are model formulas reported?
- [ ] Are exclusions reported?
- [ ] Is analytic workflow reproducible?
- [ ] Are data/code available or stated as available?

---

## 13. Robustness & Sensitivity

- [ ] Are alternative specifications tested?
- [ ] Are influential cases examined?
- [ ] Is multicollinearity checked?
- [ ] Are results stable under alternative operationalizations?

---

# V. Interpretation Discipline

## 14. Claim Calibration

- [ ] Are claims proportional to effect size?
- [ ] Are causal inferences avoided if design is non-experimental?
- [ ] Are alternative explanations discussed?
- [ ] Is overgeneralization avoided?
- [ ] Are null findings interpreted theoretically?

---

## 15. Mechanism vs Association

- [ ] Does the discussion avoid implying mechanism when only association was tested?
- [ ] Are mediators distinguished from confounds?
- [ ] Is directionality addressed cautiously?

---

## 16. Limitation Analysis (High-Level)

- [ ] Are methodological limitations specified?
- [ ] Are construct-level limitations specified?
- [ ] Are contextual limitations specified?
- [ ] Are measurement confounds acknowledged?
- [ ] Are statistical limitations acknowledged?

**Red flag:** Limitations section contains generic boilerplate.

---

# VI. Journal-Level Calibration (Example: TRSE)

## 17. Disciplinary Anchoring

- [ ] Is the manuscript clearly about disciplinary reasoning?
- [ ] Is theory of history education foregrounded?
- [ ] Are epistemic norms discussed?
- [ ] Is contextualization defined conceptually?
- [ ] Is instrument framed as measuring disciplinary competence?

---

## 18. Contribution Type (Explicit)

- [ ] Boundary condition clarification?
- [ ] Construct validity refinement?
- [ ] Theoretical integration?
- [ ] Measurement fairness?
- [ ] Instrument stress test?

Contribution must be named explicitly.

---

## 19. Tone Control

Avoid:

- alarmist rhetoric
- political moralization
- activist framing

Prefer:

- robustness
- construct clarity
- boundary specification
- epistemic constraint

---

# VII. Ethical & Transparency Standards

## 20. Ethical Compliance

- [ ] Ethics approval stated?
- [ ] Consent procedure described?
- [ ] Anonymity guaranteed?
- [ ] Sensitive content justified?
- [ ] Risk mitigation described?

---

## 21. Disclosure & Funding

- [ ] Funding sources declared?
- [ ] Conflict of interest statement included?
- [ ] Author roles clarified (if required)?

---

# VIII. Writing Quality & Technical Precision

## 22. Structural Clarity

- [ ] Logical flow between sections?
- [ ] No redundancy?
- [ ] No conceptual drift?
- [ ] Clear signposting?
- [ ] Abstract mirrors argument?

---

## 23. Terminological Consistency

- [ ] Same construct names used consistently?
- [ ] Abbreviations defined once?
- [ ] No construct inflation?
- [ ] POP/CONT/ROA consistently interpreted?

---

## 24. Tables & Figures

- [ ] Tables interpretable standalone?
- [ ] Figures labeled clearly?
- [ ] No duplication of information?
- [ ] Statistical notation correct?
- [ ] Units consistent?

---

# IX. Advanced Validity Audit (High-Level)

## 25. Construct Validity Threats

- [ ] Content validity threats addressed?
- [ ] Motivational confounds addressed?
- [ ] Social desirability controlled?
- [ ] Knowledge confound controlled?
- [ ] Ideological variance examined?

---

## 26. Internal Validity Threats

- [ ] Selection bias?
- [ ] Measurement error?
- [ ] Omitted variable bias?
- [ ] Reverse causality?
- [ ] Shared method variance?

---

## 27. External Validity Threats

- [ ] Cultural specificity acknowledged?
- [ ] Scenario specificity acknowledged?
- [ ] Age specificity acknowledged?
- [ ] Track-specific sampling discussed?

---

# X. Final Diagnostic Scoring

Rate each (1–5):

- Theoretical coherence: \_\_
- Measurement rigor: \_\_
- Statistical robustness: \_\_
- Claim restraint: \_\_
- Journal alignment: \_\_
- Reproducibility transparency: \_\_

Any dimension below 4 → revise before submission.

---

# XI. Ultra-Condensed “Reject Risk” Indicators

High probability rejection if:

- [ ] Contribution unclear.
- [ ] Theory shallow.
- [ ] Measurement validity untested.
- [ ] Null findings untheorized.
- [ ] Claims exceed design.
- [ ] Reads as technical note without disciplinary integration.
