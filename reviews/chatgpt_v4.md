## 2. Rationale (Methodologically and Theoretically Grounded)

### A. Theoretical Anchoring Is Adequate but Not Yet TRSE-Level

TRSE expects:

- explicit theorization of **disciplinary norms**
- engagement with **epistemic constraint**
- clarification of what counts as _historical contextualization_

Your manuscript:

- correctly situates HPT within contextualization and presentism debates
- introduces motivated cognition as a plausible threat
- frames ideological heterogeneity as a validity concern

However, what remains underdeveloped:

- You do not sufficiently specify **why contextualization should resist ideological alignment if it is genuinely disciplinary reasoning**.
- The epistemic mechanism of robustness (why contamination _did not_ occur) is not theoretically unpacked.
- “Validity” is discussed primarily in measurement terms, not as a property of disciplinary inference.

At TRSE, this will likely trigger the question:

> What does this study teach us about the nature of historical reasoning itself?

---

### B. The Null Result Is Under-Theorized

Your finding: no contamination effect.

Currently interpreted as:

- evidence of robustness
- reassurance about instrument validity

This is insufficient for TRSE.

A null result here should prompt theoretical reflection:

Possible explanations you must explicitly analyze:

1. **Task constraint hypothesis**
   The scenario may structurally constrain plausible interpretations so strongly that ideological projection becomes implausible.

2. **Norm internalization hypothesis**
   Students may have internalized anti-Nazi norms as part of schooling, suppressing ideological expression in disciplinary tasks.

3. **Measurement range hypothesis**
   Restricted ideological variance may attenuate contamination effects.

4. **Dual-process separation hypothesis**
   Ideological endorsement and contextual reasoning may operate through partially independent cognitive systems.

Currently, the manuscript acknowledges alternative explanations but does not systematically adjudicate among them.

TRSE reviewers will expect you to theorize these mechanisms.

---

### C. Method–Theory Integration Is Uneven

Strengths:

- Multilevel modeling appropriate for nested classroom structure.
- DIF and invariance analysis methodologically justified.
- Knowledge control included.

Weaknesses:

- Multilevel models are not explicitly interpreted as modeling classroom interpretive communities.
- Measurement invariance is treated as statistical comparability, not epistemic fairness.
- Knowledge control is not conceptually framed (background schema vs ideological override).

At TRSE, analytic decisions must be epistemically interpreted.

---

### D. Contribution Statement Needs Sharpening

Currently, contribution is implicitly framed as:

> HPT appears robust under ideological variation.

TRSE requires something more conceptually precise, such as:

- Clarification of the boundary conditions of contextualization
- Specification of limits of ideological projection in disciplinary tasks
- Evidence that contextual reasoning operates under epistemic constraints independent of ideological congruence

Without that sharpening, reviewers may interpret the study as primarily psychometric.

---

### E. Tone Calibration

The manuscript is mostly disciplined, but remnants of the following framing remain:

- “contamination”
- “inflated scores”
- implicit teacher reassurance

TRSE reviewers are sensitive to rhetoric implying:

- extremism detection
- classroom political diagnosis

Ideology must remain strictly a theoretical construct threat, not a social alarm.

## 4. What Specifically Must Be Revised Before Submission

### 1. Deepen the Theoretical Section

Add a subsection:

> Epistemic Constraints in Contextualized Historical Reasoning

Clarify:

- What makes contextualization epistemically disciplined?
- Why ideological congruence is a plausible but not inevitable confound?
- What robustness implies about disciplinary cognition.

---

### 2. Expand Discussion of Null Result

Structure it explicitly:

1. Theoretical expectation
2. Empirical finding
3. Competing explanatory mechanisms
4. Boundary conditions
5. Implications for construct validity

Currently, step 3 is underdeveloped.

---

### 3. Clarify the Construct

Explicitly distinguish:

- Moral agreement with past actors
- Strategic projection
- Contextual inferential reasoning

Demonstrate that HPT items demand inferential justification rather than ideological endorsement.

---

### 4. Tighten Claims

Explicitly state:

- Context-bound (Czech schools)
- Scenario-bound (Weimar context)
- Age-bound (secondary students)
- Ideology-range-bound

---

### 5. Strengthen Contribution Paragraph

End introduction with a precise statement such as:

> This study evaluates whether contextualized historical reasoning, as operationalized in HPT, remains epistemically constrained under ideological heterogeneity, thereby clarifying the construct validity of disciplinary perspective taking.
