## Three Reframing Strategies

The manuscript as written is an educational measurement paper. Every desk simulation flagged framing mismatch. Three distinct strategies:

### Strategy A — Social psychology of education (recommended)

- Lead with: *Does identity-protective cognition operate in educational assessment of politically charged content?*
- Foreground motivated cognition theory (Kahan), stereotype threat / identity threat literature
- Move "construct-irrelevant variance" to Methods, not Introduction
- Report TOST equivalence bounds prominently in abstract
- Add Czech post-communist social identity context
- **Targets:** Social Psychology of Education, SPB

### Strategy B — Measurement validity

- Lead with: *Validating Historical Perspective Taking against ideological response bias*
- Frame DIF + MG-CFA + scalar invariance as the primary contribution
- Position Czech adaptation as a co-equal contribution
- Reframe null as positive validity evidence ("scores reflect disciplinary thinking, not ideology")
- Add generalizable measurement principle for assessment designers
- **Targets:** AME, EPM, IJT, PARE

### Strategy C — History education

- Lead with: *Does HPT as a disciplinary practice hold up when tested against students' own political commitments?*
- Ground in Lee's understanding/endorsement distinction and Geschichtsdidaktik tradition
- Translate all statistical vocabulary to plain-language claims
- Develop the Czech Weimar scenario context for history education audience
- **Targets:** Historical Encounters, HERJ, JSSE
