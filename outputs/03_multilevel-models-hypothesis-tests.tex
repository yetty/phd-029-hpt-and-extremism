\hypertarget{purpose-and-hypotheses}{%
\section{1. Purpose and hypotheses}\label{purpose-and-hypotheses}}

This document runs the \textbf{main confirmatory analyses} for:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{H1.} Higher right‐authoritarian / pro-Nazi attitudes predict
  \textbf{higher HPT scores} on the original instrument (risk of
  ideological contamination). Predictors: FR-LF-mini (total or RD/NS
  facets) and KSA-3.
\item
  \textbf{H2.} The H1 effect \textbf{persists controlling} for prior
  knowledge (KN total) and social desirability (SDR-5).
\end{itemize}

Notes on constructs and scoring:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  HPT instrument and subscores (POP, ROA, CONT) follow Hartmann \&
  Hasselhorn / Huijgen et al.~For this file we use \textbf{HPT total,
  CONT, POP} as DVs. (In our dataset, higher is coded as ``fits Hannes's
  situation better'' across items; thus \textbf{higher CONT} means more
  contextualized alignment; \textbf{higher POP} reflects stronger
  endorsement of the presentism-trigger statements after our recoding.)
\item
  FR-LF-mini uses \textbf{RD1--RD3} (right-dictatorship) and
  \textbf{NS1--NS3} (Nazi relativization) with robust reliability and a
  validated 6-dimension parent scale. We analyze \textbf{total} and the
  \textbf{RD/NS facets}.
\item
  KSA-3 (9 items; aggression, submission, conventionalism) is included
  as a convergent authoritarian predictor. (Registered.)
\end{itemize}

\hypertarget{model-plan}{%
\section{3. Model plan}\label{model-plan}}

We estimate \textbf{random‐intercept multilevel models} (students nested
in classes). For each DV:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Base (FR-LF total):}
  \texttt{DV\ \textasciitilde{}\ z\_frlf\_tot\ +\ z\_ksa3\_tot\ +\ z\_kn\_total\ +\ z\_sdr5\_tot\ +\ (1\ \textbar{}\ class)}
\item
  \textbf{Facet (RD/NS):}
  \texttt{DV\ \textasciitilde{}\ z\_frlf\_rd\ +\ z\_frlf\_ns\ +\ z\_ksa3\_tot\ +\ z\_kn\_total\ +\ z\_sdr5\_tot\ +\ (1\ \textbar{}\ class)}
\item
  \textbf{Interaction (if preregistered):}
  \texttt{DV\ \textasciitilde{}\ z\_frlf\_tot\ *\ z\_kn\_total\ +\ z\_ksa3\_tot\ +\ z\_sdr5\_tot\ +\ (1\ \textbar{}\ class)}
\end{itemize}

DVs: \texttt{z\_hpt\_total}, \texttt{z\_hpt\_cont},
\texttt{z\_hpt\_pop}.

Interpretation (fixed effects): positive \(\beta\) means \textbf{higher
predictor → higher DV} (in SD units). For \textbf{H1--H2}, the key test
is \textbf{FR-LF coefficients} (or RD/NS) remaining positive and
significant \textbf{after controls}.

\hypertarget{results-decision-rules}{%
\section{4. Results --- decision rules}\label{results-decision-rules}}

Interpret \textbf{only the preregistered tests}:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{H1 supported} if the coefficient for \textbf{FR-LF} (either
  \texttt{z\_frlf\_tot} in Base/Int. or
  \texttt{z\_frlf\_rd}/\texttt{z\_frlf\_ns} in Facet) is \textbf{\(>\) 0
  and \(p < .05\)} for \textbf{HPT total} and/or \textbf{CONT}.
\item
  \textbf{H2 supported} if the same holds \textbf{after} adding controls
  (\textbf{KN}, \textbf{SDR-5}) and \textbf{KSA-3} (already included),
  and --- if preregistered --- the \textbf{FR-LF × KN} interaction is
  \textbf{not necessary} for the main effect to persist (or, if
  hypothesized, is significant in the expected direction).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Reading POP.} Given our recoding (1--4 ``fit''), higher
\textbf{POP} here reflects stronger agreement that the
presentism-trigger statements ``fit'' Hannes. In the original
instrument, POP and CONT came out as a single factor vs.~ROA in CFA, and
item wording can trip respondents; interpret POP cautiously and
triangulate with CONT.

\hypertarget{brief-interpretation-guide-for-the-write-up}{%
\section{5. Brief interpretation guide (for the
write-up)}\label{brief-interpretation-guide-for-the-write-up}}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Effect size:} Coefficients are \textbf{standardized}
  (\(\beta\)). Values around 0.10 are small, 0.20--0.30 moderate for
  individual-level predictors in multilevel models; report 95\% CIs.
\item
  \textbf{Clustering:} Report \textbf{ICC} to show class-level variance.
\item
  \textbf{Model fit:} Report marginal and conditional \(R^2\) and
  compare Base vs.~Facet vs.~Interaction.
\item
  \textbf{Substantive meaning:} A \textbf{positive FR-LF} effect on
  \textbf{HPT total / CONT} suggests that ideological affinity
  \textbf{elevates apparent contextualization}, consistent with the
  contamination concern. Cite the HPT literature and the FR-LF
  validation when interpreting.
\item
  \textbf{Controls:} If FR-LF remains significant after \textbf{KN} and
  \textbf{SDR-5}, state that results are \textbf{not explained} by prior
  knowledge or social desirability (per H2).
\end{itemize}
