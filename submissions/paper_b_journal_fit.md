# Paper B Journal-Fit Analysis

**Date:** 2026-03-23
**Paper:** "Cross-Cultural Validation and Ideological Fairness of a Historical Perspective Taking Instrument: Evidence from Czech Secondary Students"
**MS ID (rejected):** HAME-2026-0087 (AME)
**Method:** Deep scope research + 3-editor desk-decision simulation per journal

## Iteration 1: 6 Journals Evaluated

### Journals Passing All 3 Editor Simulations (Unanimous SEND TO REVIEW)

---

#### 1. International Journal of Testing (IJT)

**Publisher:** Taylor & Francis | **IF:** ~1.0 | **Review:** Double-blind, 3 referees
**Editor-suggested by:** AME rejection letter (Joel Puchalla)
**CzechELib:** Yes (T&F Read & Publish, 52 tokens for CU in 2026)

**Why it fits:**
- Cross-cultural test adaptation is IJT's **core mission** — explicitly named in scope
- DIF and measurement invariance are standard IJT topics
- Test fairness with ideology as grouping variable is novel for IJT
- Domain (history education) is unusual but acceptable — IJT is domain-agnostic
- Preregistration + OSF replication package align with IJT's open science direction

**Editor panel highlights:**
- All 3 editors agreed the CIV-ideology framing elevates this above routine validation
- The Czech adaptation of a German instrument is textbook IJT content
- N=293 is adequate for the analytic pipeline

**Reviewer concerns to prepare for:**
- Statistical power for DIF detection (N=293 split by ideology groups)
- CFI=.999 scrutiny (near-saturated model with 9 items, 3 factors)
- Accounting for clustered data (students in classrooms in schools)
- Generalizability (one scenario, one country)
- Framing: what do we learn beyond "the adaptation worked"?

**Verdict: STRONG FIT — PRIMARY TARGET**

---

#### 2. Journal of Psychoeducational Assessment (JPA)

**Publisher:** SAGE | **IF:** ~1.5 | **Review:** Peer-reviewed
**CzechELib:** Check SAGE agreement availability

**Why it fits:**
- JPA explicitly welcomes: cross-cultural assessment, DIF, validity studies of new and often-used tests
- "Carefully conducted test adaptations that report additional validity evidence" are specifically invited
- HPT is the most widely used standardized HPT measure in European research — qualifies as a notable instrument
- History education is "psychoeducational" in the broad sense
- Fairness/CIV framing makes it accessible to JPA's psychology readership

**Editor panel highlights:**
- All 3 editors passed it, though Editor 2 (school psychology) noted HPT is peripheral to JPA's typical content
- The fairness angle was the deciding factor — it generalizes beyond history to any politically charged assessment
- Validity theory framing (Messick, Kane) resonates with JPA's mission

**Reviewer concerns to prepare for:**
- Why should JPA readers care about HPT specifically? Must argue generalizability
- Clustered data handling (multilevel CFA or robust SEs)
- Convergent/discriminant validity — reviewers may want additional construct correlations
- Practical implications for assessment practice
- Ideology measurement quality and group formation

**Verdict: STRONG FIT — SECONDARY TARGET**

---

#### 3. Psychological Test Adaptation and Development (PTAD)

**Publisher:** Hogrefe (Open Access, no APC) | **Indexed:** PsycINFO, Scopus
**Official journal of:** European Association of Psychological Assessment
**CzechELib:** N/A — fully open access, free to publish

**Why it fits:**
- PTAD exists **specifically** for test adaptation studies — this is its exact mission
- German→Czech adaptation with full psychometric evaluation is textbook PTAD content
- Preregistration strongly encouraged by PTAD — this study is preregistered
- Open data/replication package aligns perfectly
- Fairness analysis (ideology as CIV) adds novel contribution beyond routine adaptation
- European sample, European instrument origin, European author

**Editor panel highlights:**
- All 3 editors agreed scope is unambiguous — "this is what PTAD was created for"
- Concern about HPT being "educational" rather than "psychological" was raised but dismissed — perspective taking has deep psychological roots (Piaget, Selman, Davis)
- Instrument's limited international visibility flagged — reviewers may want justification for why this instrument merits adaptation

**Reviewer concerns to prepare for:**
- Audience framing: foreground the generalizable method (ideology as CIV source), not just history education
- Power analysis for DIF detection
- Cultural equivalence of Weimar Republic scenario (Czech vs. German context)
- "Everything works perfectly" skepticism (CFI=.999, 0/9 DIF, full scalar invariance)
- Practical utility of Czech adaptation — who will use it?

**Verdict: EXCELLENT FIT — SAFEST OPTION (zero APC, lowest desk-reject risk)**

### Journal Passing by Majority (2/3 Editors)

#### 4. Measurement: Interdisciplinary Research and Perspectives

**Publisher:** Taylor & Francis | **IF:** ~1.2
**CzechELib:** Yes (T&F)

**Split:** 2 send to review, 1 soft desk reject (encouragement to resubmit with stronger CIV framing)

**Why it partially fits:**
- Interdisciplinary measurement journal covering social sciences
- CIV-ideology question qualifies as "advance in measurement applications"
- History education meets psychometric fairness = interdisciplinary

**Why it partially doesn't:**
- One editor felt it reads as cross-cultural validation with appended fairness analysis
- Would need restructuring: lead with CIV-from-ideology as general measurement question, HPT as case study
- Null finding harder to sell without power analysis

**Verdict: POSSIBLE BUT REQUIRES REFRAMING — not recommended over IJT/JPA/PTAD**

### Journals Failing (Majority Desk Reject)

#### EJPA (European Journal of Psychological Assessment) — REJECTED 2/3

**Key reasons:** HPT is educational, not psychological. Instrument is niche (9 items, α=.47–.64). Adaptation serves a tiny research community. "EJPA receives dozens of 'we translated X into Y' papers annually" — this one lacks sufficient psychological substance.

#### EPM (Educational and Psychological Measurement) — REJECTED 2/3

**Key reasons:** Methodologically competent but incremental. No new method proposed. Null findings on fairness are important but not novel enough for EPM's ~15–20% acceptance rate. "Neither a groundbreaking validation nor a definitive fairness study."

### Strategic Notes

1. **Before submitting to IJT, address recurring reviewer concerns:**
   - Add a power/sensitivity analysis for DIF detection
   - Explicitly address clustered data handling (report design effects or use TYPE=COMPLEX)
   - Strengthen the Discussion's generalizability argument (CIV from ideology in any scenario-based assessment)
   - Prepare for "CFI=.999" scrutiny — explain the near-saturated model

2. **Reframing for IJT:** The current manuscript leads with history education context. For IJT, the fairness/CIV question and cross-cultural adaptation angle should be more prominent. Mention in cover letter: "As suggested by the editorial office of Applied Measurement in Education..."

3. **Do NOT submit to:** Educational Assessment (explicit exclusion of validation studies unless methodologically novel), AME (already rejected), EJPA (educational instrument mismatch), EPM (contribution too incremental).
